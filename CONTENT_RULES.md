# Content Rules

## Source and Originality

- Do not copy text from Wiki, Fandom, Steam Guides, or other guide sites.
- Do not paraphrase source material so closely that it becomes a rewritten copy.
- Write original strategy guidance based on player problems, decision logic, and verified or clearly marked data.

## Verification Rules

- Do not invent unverified prices, levels, times, seasons, fish locations, merchants, recipe values, unlock requirements, or exact mechanics.
- Any data that has not been manually confirmed must be marked as `needs_verification`.
- If exact values matter for a route, tell the player to verify them in the current game version.
- It is acceptable to give strategy advice, priority logic, checklists, and decision routes without exact numbers.

## Sitemap Rules

- Stub pages must not enter the sitemap.
- `needs_verification` database pages should not enter the sitemap.
- Verified or completed pages are suitable for sitemap inclusion.
- Strategy pages can enter the sitemap when they provide original, useful decision guidance.

## Required Guide Structure

Every strategy guide must answer:

1. Who is this for?
2. Quick answer
3. What should the player do next?
4. What should the player avoid?
5. Related tools
6. Related database pages
7. FAQ

Guides should not read like generic AI articles. They should solve a specific player problem, explain why the recommendation works, give alternatives when relevant, and point to the next useful page.

## Required Database Detail Strategy Block

Every database detail page must include a strategy block with:

- Best used for
- Good for beginners?
- Stockpile priority
- Works well with
- Avoid using when
- Related planner suggestions

Database detail pages should connect raw item information to practical tavern decisions. A recipe page should help players decide when to put it on the menu. An ingredient page should help players decide whether to reserve, spend, stockpile, or avoid using it.

## Writing Rules

- Prefer practical advice over encyclopedic description.
- Use player-facing questions as the organizing logic.
- Avoid unsupported superlatives such as "best" unless the recommendation is framed by conditions.
- Do not make fixed profit claims without verified values.
- Preserve `needs_verification` when data has not been confirmed.
- Link tools, strategy guides, and database pages together so the player can continue planning.
